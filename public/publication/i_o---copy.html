Using Input-output analysis to analyze the economy of the grande de San Ramón

Cardoza David, Vargas Juan, Briceño Rodrigo, Vargas Henry

Resumen

Input Output analysis is an important tool for understanding the economy of any region or country. It was originally designed during World War II by the Russian Nobel laureate Wassily Leontief, then working in the United States, to make sure that when a tank or similar machine was being built, the final product would not be held up by the lack of some small part-for example, the right kind of washer for a tread.
